## üîó WHERE TO FIND SOURCES

### Recommended Databases
- **PubMed** (pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) - Pregnancy and obstetrics research
- **Google Scholar** (scholar.google.com) - Academic papers
- **Cochrane Library** (cochranelibrary.com) - Systematic reviews of pregnancy interventions
- **ClinicalTrials.gov** - Pregnancy-related clinical trials

### Reputable Organizations to Cite

**Tier 1 (Best - Always Acceptable):**
- American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG) - acog.org
- March of Dimes - marchofdimes.org
- National Institutes of Health (NIH) - nih.gov
- Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) - cdc.gov
- World Health Organization (WHO) pregnancy guidelines

**Tier 2 (Good - Medical Institutions):**
- Mayo Clinic - mayoclinic.org
- Cleveland Clinic - clevelandclinic.org
- Johns Hopkins Medicine - hopkinsmedicine.org
- American Pregnancy Association - americanpregnancy.org

**Tier 3 (Use with Caution):**
- What to Expect, The Bump, BabyCenter - only if no better source available and for general information only

### How to Add Citations to Blog

When you find sources, add them at the end of relevant paragraphs like this:

```html
<p>Research suggests that women who actively monitor their pregnancy health report better outcomes.<sup><a href="[URL]" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[1]</a></sup></p>
```

Then expand the References section at the bottom with full citations:

```html
<h2>References</h2>
<ol class="references">
    <li>Author Name, et al. "Study Title." Journal of Maternal-Fetal Medicine. Year. <a href="[URL]" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Link</a></li>
    <li>American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists. "Practice Bulletin: Title." ACOG. Year. <a href="[URL]" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Link</a></li>
</ol>
```
