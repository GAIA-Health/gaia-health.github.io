## Why This Series

The period tracker comparison post drives 82% of all search impressions. It proves that honest, structured app comparisons attract high-intent users who are actively deciding which app to download. Condition-specific comparisons target **higher-intent, lower-competition** queries where we can rank faster and convert better.

### What Makes the Existing Comparison Post Work

1. **Transparency disclaimer** up front ("This guide is published by Holland Neurotech Inc.")
2. **3-step decision framework** (Identify needs → Understand options → Make decision)
3. **Standardized app profiles** (Best for, Features, Strengths, Limitations, Pricing)
4. **Emoji comparison table** (quick scanning with checkmarks, warnings, locks, X marks)
5. **Honest about Go Go Gaia's limitations** (iOS-only, newer app, smaller community)
6. **Genuine competitor praise** (Clue's science credibility, Flo's content library)
7. **Decision matrix** at the end ("Choose X if...")
8. **Go Go Gaia positioned as all-in-one**, not "the best at everything"

**Every new comparison post must replicate this exact structure and tone.**

- Pricing: Free version (good), Premium ~$34.99/year

**5. PCOS Tracker (by Applied Informatics)**
- Position: PCOS-specific dedicated app
- What users say (recent): Helpful for logging PCOS-specific symptoms (hair loss, acne, excess hair growth, weight). But user reviews note limitations with handling the very irregular cycles that are common in PCOS.
- Strengths: Built specifically for PCOS, tracks PCOS-specific symptoms that general apps miss
- Honest limitations: Limited to PCOS symptoms only, no mood, nutrition, fitness, or broader health tracking. Smaller development team with slower updates. No wearable integration. No correlation insights.
- Pricing: Free (basic), in-app purchases

**6. AskPCOS (by Monash University)**
- Position: Most clinically credible PCOS education app
- What users say: Co-designed with PCOS patients by the researchers who wrote the international PCOS guidelines
- Strengths: Completely free, evidence-based, doctor appointment prep tools, question prompt lists
- Honest limitations: Primarily educational (basic symptom tracker), last updated Sept 2023, very few App Store ratings, no nutrition/fitness/wearable features

## Tone & Voice Reminders (from TONE-OF-VOICE-GUIDE.md)

- **Sound like:** Talking to a friend who's smart and curious about their health
- **Be:** Direct, conversational, honest, specific, respectful
- **Forbidden words:** "finally," "seamlessly," "transform," "journey," "empower," "game-changer," "unlock," "elevate," "holistic," "comprehensive" (both "holistic" and "comprehensive" should be replaced with specifics about what the app actually does)
- **Sentence structure:** Use contractions, vary length, start with action, active voice
- **Red flags:** "we're excited to," "in today's world," "whether you're"
- **NO em-dashes** anywhere in content. Use periods, commas, or colons instead.
- **NO semicolons.** Use periods instead.
- **The test:** Read it aloud. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.

### Competitor Respect Rules (learned from PCOS post)
- Frame limitations as what the user experiences, not what the competitor is doing wrong
- Use "some users note" or "some reviews mention" instead of "users complain"
- Don't use loaded words like "aggressive" to describe competitor behavior (use "frequent" instead)
- Don't use snarky asides (e.g., "which is ironic for a PCOS app")
- Our USP is being all-in-one. Lead with what we offer, not what competitors lack
- Don't stack superlatives ("most comprehensive," "no other app," "unique"). State facts flatly
