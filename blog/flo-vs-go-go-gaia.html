The Real Difference: Focused Tracker vs All-in-One

Here's the part that decides most of this comparison, so it's worth saying plainly before the feature lists.

Flo is built around your cycle. Everything centers on period and fertility tracking, and it does that job extremely well, with predictions, a deep content library, and a community of millions. You can log mood and symptoms too, but the cycle is the heart of the app.

Go Go Gaia is built around your whole health. Your cycle is one piece, sitting next to mood, sleep, nutrition, fitness, and habits, with the app surfacing how they connect. The trade is breadth for a smaller community and, for now, iOS only.

So the real question isn't "which app is better." It's whether you want a focused cycle tracker with the biggest community and content behind it, or a broader tracker that ties your cycle to the rest of your day. Both are valid. They just suit different people.

Flo: What You Get

Flo is the most downloaded period tracker in the world, with over 420 million downloads, and it earned that spot. It's polished, easy to use, and genuinely one of the best focused cycle trackers you can install. For a lot of people, Flo is all they need, and that's a fair conclusion to reach.

Features

  • Cycle and period tracking with AI-powered predictions
  • Fertile window and ovulation estimates (conception mode)
  • Thousands of health articles reviewed by medical professionals
  • AI health assistant (Flo Premium)
  • Anonymous community discussions (Secret Chats) with millions of users
  • Free pregnancy mode with week-by-week tracking
  • Apple Health integration and an Apple Watch companion app
  • Anonymous Mode for logging without linking data to your identity

Strengths

The community and content are the biggest in the space. Flo has thousands of expert-reviewed articles and anonymous discussions with millions of other users. If you want to learn while you track, or just feel less alone with a symptom, very little comes close.

The free tier is genuinely usable. You can track your cycle, log symptoms, get predictions, and use pregnancy mode without paying. For many people, free Flo is plenty.

It's polished and on every phone. Flo is simple, well designed, and available on both iOS and Android, so it works no matter what you carry. Predictions are reliable for regular cycles after a few months of logging.

Limitations

It's centered on the cycle. If you want to track mood, sleep, nutrition, and habits in real depth and see how they connect, that isn't what Flo is built around. It logs some of those, but the cycle is the focus.

Privacy history. Flo settled with the FTC in 2021 over sharing health data, and a class action over the same issue followed in 2025. Flo has since added Anonymous Mode. More on this in the privacy section.

Ads and paywalls. Free users see ads, and the AI assistant plus deeper insights sit behind Flo Premium.

Pricing

Free: Cycle and symptom tracking, predictions, pregnancy mode, community. Includes ads.

Flo Premium: ~$39.99/year. Adds the AI assistant, an ad-free experience, and advanced insights.

Download: Available on iOS and Android

Go Go Gaia: What You Get

Go Go Gaia is the app we make, so take this section knowing that. It's a newer, smaller app than Flo, and it's built for a different goal: tracking your whole health in one place instead of just your cycle. It's the right pick for some people and the wrong pick for others, and we'll be straight about both.

Features

  • Cycle tracking with automatic phase detection and fertility predictions
  • Mood, sleep, fitness, and nutrition tracking built in
  • Custom habit tracking with auto-updating streaks
  • Correlation insights showing how your lifestyle affects your cycle and mood
  • Wearable sync with Apple Watch, Oura Ring, and Garmin
  • Pregnancy mode, plus PCOS and perimenopause support
  • No ads, no data selling

Strengths

It's all in one place. Instead of one app for your cycle, another for food, and a third for mood, Go Go Gaia puts them together and shows the connections, like how your sleep affects your mood in the luteal phase. That's the main reason to choose it over a focused tracker.

The free tier is broad and ad-free. Cycle, mood, sleep, fitness, nutrition, and habits are all in the free tier, with no ads, which is a genuine difference for an app holding this kind of data.

Wearable sync is built in. If you have an Apple Watch, Oura Ring, or Garmin, that data connects to your cycle and the rest of your tracking.

Limitations

We'll be honest about where Flo wins:

  • iOS only. There's no Android app yet. A web app is available, but if you're on Android, Flo is the practical choice.
  • No community. Flo's discussions with millions of users are a real strength, and Go Go Gaia doesn't have an in-app community like that.
  • Smaller content library. Flo's expert-reviewed library is far larger than ours.
  • Newer and less proven. Fewer reviews and a shorter track record than Flo's years in the market.
  • AI features are premium. The AI assistant (Ask Gaia) requires a subscription.

Pricing

Free: Cycle, mood, sleep, fitness, nutrition, and habit tracking. No ads.

Premium: ~$9.99/month or ~$49.99/year for AI features and advanced insights.

Which Should You Choose?

There's no single "best" app here, because they're built for different priorities. Here's the honest breakdown:

Choose Flo if:

  • You want a focused, polished period tracker and don't need it to do more
  • A large community and the biggest content library matter to you
  • You're on Android, or you want an app that's on every platform
  • You want free pregnancy tracking with a proven, established app
  • Your main goal is knowing what your cycle is going to do

Choose Go Go Gaia if:

  • You want cycle, mood, sleep, fitness, nutrition, and habits in one app
  • You track with a wearable and want that data connected to your cycle
  • You want to see how your lifestyle affects your cycle and symptoms
  • You want a generous free tier with no ads
  • You're on iPhone and don't need an in-app community

If you're still torn, there's no harm in trying both. They each have a free tier, so you can track a cycle or two in each and keep whichever fits the way you actually use it.

Privacy: Worth a Close Look

Cycle and health data is some of the most sensitive information you can put in an app, so privacy deserves real attention with both.

Flo: A Complicated History, With Real Changes

In 2021, the FTC settled with Flo over sharing users' health data with companies including Facebook and Google without proper consent. In 2025, Flo and Google agreed to a $56 million settlement, part of a roughly $59.5 million fund resolving related claims, and a jury separately found Meta liable in August 2025 for its role in receiving that data.

Since then, Flo has added Anonymous Mode, which separates your health data from your identity, and it has open-sourced that work. Those are real steps, and Flo has years of public scrutiny behind it. Whether the history changes your decision is personal, but it's worth knowing.

Go Go Gaia: Privacy-Focused, Smaller Scale

Go Go Gaia doesn't run ads and doesn't sell user data, and you can export or delete your data. It's a newer, smaller, US-based company without the years of public scrutiny the bigger names have been through, and there's no GDPR certification. The no-ads, no-data-selling approach is a meaningful commitment, just at a smaller scale and with a shorter track record.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Go Go Gaia a good alternative to Flo?

It depends on what you want from the app. Flo is the more established choice, with a huge community, the largest content library in the space, and apps on both iOS and Android. Go Go Gaia is a good alternative if you want to track more than your cycle in one place, since it covers mood, sleep, nutrition, fitness, and habits alongside your cycle and shows how they connect. If you mainly want a focused period tracker with content and community, Flo is hard to beat. If you want an all-in-one tracker with a generous free tier and wearable sync, Go Go Gaia is worth a look, as long as you are on iPhone.

What does Go Go Gaia do that Flo doesn't?

The main difference is scope. Go Go Gaia tracks your cycle plus mood, sleep, nutrition, fitness, and custom habits in one app, then surfaces correlations between them, like how your sleep affects your mood in the luteal phase. It also syncs with Apple Watch, Oura Ring, and Garmin for things like temperature and activity. Flo can track mood and symptoms too, but it is built around the cycle as its center, while Go Go Gaia is built to be a whole-health tracker. Flo, on the other hand, has community, content, and Android, which Go Go Gaia does not.

Does Go Go Gaia work on Android?

Not yet. Go Go Gaia is iOS only right now, with a web app available, but no native Android version. Flo is on both iOS and Android. So if you use an Android phone, Flo is the practical choice today, and Go Go Gaia would only fit if you are on an iPhone or mainly use the web app.

Is Go Go Gaia free like Flo?

Both have a free tier. Flo's free tier is genuinely usable for cycle tracking and includes ads, with Flo Premium at about $39.99 per year for the AI assistant and an ad-free experience. Go Go Gaia's free tier covers cycle, mood, sleep, fitness, nutrition, and habit tracking with no ads, and its premium, at about $9.99 per month or $49.99 per year, adds AI features and advanced insights. So both are free to start. The difference is that Go Go Gaia's free tier is ad-free and broader in scope, while Flo's free tier is backed by the largest content library and community.

Is Flo or Go Go Gaia better for privacy?

They take different approaches. Flo settled with the FTC in 2021 over sharing health data, and in 2025 Flo and Google agreed to a $56 million settlement that is part of a roughly $59.5 million fund, with a jury separately finding Meta liable in August 2025. Flo has since added Anonymous Mode, which separates your health data from your identity, and open-sourced it, and it has years of public scrutiny behind it. Go Go Gaia is a smaller, US-based app that does not run ads or sell user data, but it has not been through that same level of public scrutiny and has no GDPR certification. Read both current policies and decide what you are comfortable with.

Does Go Go Gaia have a community like Flo?

No. Community is one of Flo's biggest strengths, with anonymous discussions among millions of users, and Go Go Gaia does not have an in-app community like that. If talking with other people and reading a large library of expert-reviewed articles is part of why you use a tracker, Flo offers far more there. Go Go Gaia focuses on your own data and the connections within it rather than on a social experience.
