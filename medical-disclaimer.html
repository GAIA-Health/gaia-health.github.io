Always consult a qualified healthcare provider

Before making any decisions about your health — including starting, stopping, or changing any treatment, medication, supplement, diet, or exercise program — speak with a licensed healthcare provider who knows your medical history.

This is especially important if you:

  • Are pregnant, trying to conceive, or postpartum
  • Are managing a chronic condition such as PCOS, endometriosis, PMDD, perimenopause, thyroid disease, or an autoimmune condition
  • Take prescription medication or hormonal contraception
  • Have any new, severe, persistent, or unusual symptoms

Never disregard professional medical advice or delay seeking it because of something you read on this site or in the app.

Medical emergencies

If you think you are experiencing a medical emergency, do not rely on the Service.

  • In the United States, call 911 or go to your nearest emergency department.
  • In the UK, call 999 or 111.
  • In other regions, contact your local emergency number.
  • For mental health crises in the US, call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline).

Examples of symptoms that need urgent care include heavy bleeding, severe abdominal or pelvic pain, chest pain, signs of stroke, signs of severe pregnancy complications, reduced fetal movement, or thoughts of self-harm.

Go Go Gaia is a tracking tool, not a medical device

Go Go Gaia helps you log and visualize cycle data, symptoms, mood, habits, and other health inputs. Predictions (such as expected period dates, fertile windows, or pregnancy week estimates) are statistical estimates based on the data you enter. They are not diagnoses, they are not contraceptive guidance, and they should not be used as the sole basis for any medical decision.

Go Go Gaia is not a contraceptive, fertility treatment, or diagnostic device, and it has not been evaluated by the FDA, MHRA, or any other regulatory body as a medical device.
